The artist
ジェレミー・クリーシー

Drawn to the essential,
not the elaborate

Jeremy Creasey is a British artist whose practice is shaped by years of immersion in Japanese aesthetics — the philosophy of ma (negative space), the imperfect beauty of wabi-sabi, and the meditative discipline of sumi-e ink work.

Working primarily in sumi ink on handmade washi paper, Jeremy's originals carry the quiet authority of works that resist rush. Each brushstroke is deliberate and irreversible — there is no undo. Prints are produced as limited giclée editions on archival papers, faithful to the texture and tone of the originals.

Portrait commissions are a central part of Jeremy's practice. Whether capturing a person, a beloved animal, or a meaningful place, the approach remains the same: careful observation, generous listening, and a final work that feels both true and timeless.

Portrait commissions

A portrait is a conversation
with time

Every portrait commission begins with listening. Jeremy works closely with each client to understand not just what a subject looks like, but what a piece should feel like when it's lived with — on a wall, for decades.

From intimate ink studies to large-format giclée portraits, each piece is produced with the same unhurried attention. Commissions are accepted for people, animals, and places.

The practice

間 — Ma (negative space)
What is left out defines what remains. Every work is as much about the empty paper as the mark upon it.
侘寂 — Wabi-sabi
The beauty of imperfection and impermanence. Ink brushed on washi cannot be undone — and that is its truth.
一期一会 — Ichi-go ichi-e
Each encounter — with a subject, a commission, a blank sheet — is singular and unrepeatable. It deserves full presence.
